MISSION
Three Conditions for the Ideal Form
“Chef-in-Residence” invites chefs from outside the region to help revitalize local areas—an important means to boost a region’s creativity and vitality. We regard traveling chefs as key to regional revitalization, and define the following three conditions as the ideal form we aim to support.
1. Continuous Rotation of Visiting Chefs Not a one-off event: throughout the year, a diverse array of chefs keeps coming, and limited-term restaurants are opened on an ongoing basis. This continuity brings sustained energy and fresh stimulation to the region.
2. Open to Both Tourists and Locals Designed not only for visitors but also for local residents to casually drop in and enjoy an extraordinary experience. The restaurant does not become isolated from the community; instead, it becomes a venue where the region, tourists, and chefs interact.
3. Systems that Integrate Chefs with the Region Mechanisms and environments that naturally connect traveling chefs with local people and ingredients—so that points of contact are created beyond the restaurant itself and everyday exchanges can emerge.

ABOUT
About the Association
A General Incorporated Association that supports Chef-in-Residence programs The Japan Chef-in-Residence Empowerment Association (General Incorporated Association) is a nonprofit that supports Chef-in-Residence activities. In particular, we support regions, chefs, and businesses striving to realize Chef-in-Residence programs that meet our three conditions: (1) continuous rotation of visiting chefs, (2) open to both local residents and tourists, and (3) mechanisms that naturally integrate chefs and the region. We aim for this form of Chef-in-Residence to spread across Japan and function as a “brackish water zone between everyday and extraordinary life”—a place that nurtures a Creative Regional Climate. Through this, we hope regions will become more vibrant and interesting, and Japan as a whole more fascinating and enriched.
What is “Chef-in-Residence”?
“Chef-in-Residence” welcomes traveling chefs from Japan and around the world into a region to operate a limited-term restaurant. The goal is to spark new encounters and creativity. As traveling chefs interact with local people, ingredients, culture, and climate, the region’s appeal is (re)discovered and amplified—while chefs gain experiences unique to that place. Our Association supports the sustainable development and wider adoption of such activities throughout Japan.
What is a “Creative Regional Climate”?
iC Model — innumerable Cs Model A Creative Regional Climate refers to the shared values, mindsets, and behavioral patterns of people in a region—an environment where creativity naturally flourishes and new challenges and interactions continuously emerge. By cultivating such an environment, communities form where people can easily try new things and grow together while inspiring one another. We believe Chef-in-Residence, functioning as a “brackish water zone between everyday and extraordinary life”*, helps elevate this creative climate.
* Brackish water zone: literally, where river and sea meet and fresh water mixes with seawater. By extension, we use it to mean spaces/opportunities where people, things, and ideas of different natures mix and interact.
Reference: Discussion Paper | The iC Model (innumerable Cs Model) for Creating a Creative Regional Climate

WHAT WE DO
1. Information Sharing & Research We research and analyze the challenges and strengths of traveling chefs, host regions, and kitchens, and share findings widely to highlight solutions and successful practices.
2. Awards Program We honor chefs, regions, and kitchens that embody the ideal Chef-in-Residence model to catalyze activity across Japan and encourage more regions to join.
3. Support for Traveling Chefs & Host Regions We help traveling chefs and host regions collaborate effectively and smoothly—for example, by advising on kitchen operations, providing practical on-site support, assisting chef stays, and facilitating international exchange by inviting foreign chefs.
4. Foreign Chef Invitation Program We work toward inviting foreign chefs to operate as traveling chefs in local regions—to assess impact and surface practical challenges.
